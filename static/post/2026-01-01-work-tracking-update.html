Tracking my work and non-work time.

Author

Sam Zipper

Published

This is the sixth edition of my time tracking analysis, adding in the 2025 calendar year.

  • 2025 Goals
  • How I do it

My goals, and associated metrics, for 2025 included:

  • Unplug more: Maintain number of weeks with 1 unplugged day (no work including checking email).
  • Sign off: Get to 15 weeks with no evening work and 20 weeks with <= 1 day of evening work.
  • Increase research time: Keep percent of time spent on research at least 15%.

I manually track the hours in a simple google sheet at 30 min resolution and divided into a few simple categories:

  • talking = things that involved other people… meetings, seminars, zoom calls, etc.
  • logistics = stuff that is generally unimportant (but sometimes necessary). emails, expense reports, etc.
  • research = writing proposals or papers, making presentations, data analysis, reading papers, etc.
  • mentees = talking to or working with someone I am supervising such as a postdoc, student, or staff, including reviewing their work
  • conference = anything at a conference/workshop.
  • service = journal or grant reviews, committees, open science stuff, etc.
  • teaching = courses/guest lectures, including prep time and grading
  • field = field work

Tracking 2025 Goals

Metric 1: More unplugged days

Interesting dynamics between my three indicators here:

  • The total number of weeks with >= 1 unplugged day remained near the maximum possible value, with 48 weeks in 2025, or 92.3% of weeks.
  • Additionally, the number of weeks with >= 2 unplugged days increased from 16 in 2024 to 21 in 2025.
  • However, the total number of unplugged days decreased by 10 days, from 88 in 2024 to 78 in 2025.

How could this happen? Well, seems like I spread my unplugged days more evenly across weeks. Comparing the past two years, in 2024 I had fewer longer chunks of consecutive unplugged days, while in 2025 I was more consistently unplugged for multiple days per week, but didn’t have as many long chunks. This shows up pretty clearly if you add one more day and look at the number of weeks with at least 3 unplugged days, which had been rising from a low of 0 weeks (in 2019 and 2020) to a high of 4 weeks (in 2024) until dropping by 50% down to 2 weeks in 2025.

Metric 2: Decrease evening work.

Major improvements here, going from 10 weeks with no evening work last year to 22 weeks this year. The number of weeks with <= 1 day/week of work in the evening nearly doubled as well, from 19 weeks in 2024 to 39 weeks in 2025. Both of these are at all-time highs for me.

As far as the drivers of evening work this year, it looks like I had a decent amount of meetings in the evening in the first half of 2025. This was largely associated with taking job candidates out for dinner, as there were a couple ongoing searches in the spring. I also had pretty irregular work hours for a couple chunks of time over the summer that led to filling in some gaps in the evenings.

Metric 3: Research >15%

Made it by a hair, with 17% of my time spent on research in 2025, once again inching up from the prior years (16% in 2024 and 15.7% in 2023).

Looking across all categories, thing have more or less stabilized:

  • Logistics: Slight increase, at 27.2% in 2025 compared to 25.5% in 2024.
  • Talking: Slight decrease, at 21.4% in 2025 compared to 23.7% in 2024. Note that this does not include meetings with mentees, which are counted separately.
  • Mentees: Slight decrease, at 17.8% in 2025 compared to 20.2% in 2024.
  • Conferences/workshops: Slight increase, at 7.6% in 2025 compared to 5.4% in 2024.

Any updates for 2026?

Most of these indicators, as well as the total work by category, looks like I’ve converged on a fairly sustainable groove as far as both how much I’m working and how the time is split among categories:

It would still be nice to get research up to a higher percentage. I’ve applied for a sabbatical in fall 2026, which would be an opportunity to re-configure things towards more research time if successful. Overall, my goals for 2026 are largely the same as for 2025, with a few tweaks:

  • At least 40 weeks with >= 1 unplugged day and at least 26 weeks with >= 2 unplugged days.
  • At least 24 weeks with no evening work and at least 40 weeks with <= 1 day of evening work.
  • Keep research time above 15%.
